TLVOTHE (discord), is a multiplayer, murder mystery, roleplay-based game in Discord that takes place on a moving train
in the 1900's called "The Harpy Express".

Players move between cabins, do tasks, investigate crimes, interact with other passengers, and try to survive from the murderer until the end of the voyage.

This game is heavily reliant on players' attention, communication, and roleplay between each other.

TLVOTHE is designed as a long-form social deduction game. While the bot manages movement, inventories, items, and other mechanics, the players themselves drive the story through roleplay, investigation, alliances, and deception.

----- CORE GAMEPLAY -----

  At the start of the game, the players are placed aboard the train, each given a key to enter their own cabin rooms and a hidden role.
  Each cabin is represented by a Discord channel in a category of the server.

  Players may:
- Move between train cars
- Speak with others nearby
- Investigate rooms
- Discover evidence
- Witness suspicious behavior
- Complete objectives
- Kill other players (depending on role)
- Hide information
- Cooperate or deceive

  The game progresses dynamically depending on player actions.

----- ROOM SYSTEM -----

  The train consists of connected rooms/train cars.

Examples:
- Cafeteria
- Lounge
- Open-air cargo cabin

Players may only see the channel representing the room they are
currently inside.

When a player moves:
- Access to the previous room is removed
- Access to the new room is granted

Players cannot scroll through previous room history, preventing
metagaming and preserving immersion.
Rooms may naturally contain items that players can discover and collect.

----- MOVEMENT SYSTEM -----

All movement in this game is directional (except for the Murderer)
  This means everyone can move to the front or the back (the murderer can go up above the train, giving access to any room) unless the target isn't available (locked doors, end of the train, locked cabins, etc.).

Movement is handled through the /move command.

----- ROLES -----

There are 3 main roles in TLVOTHE:
  - Murderer
    - The main class that kills, wins if every Passenger and Vigilante is dead, can have killer cohorts.
    - Has access to an item shop for killing items. (More info in the server, or may be added later here as a separate txt file)
  - Vigilante
    - Protect yourself and all the passengers with your might, has a Revolver,
    - Kill all the murderers with eyewitnesses and evidence.
  - Passenger
    - Survive, while doing your own personal objectives.
    - Help the Vigilante decipher who the murderer is.

The good guys (Passenger, Vigilante) win by having all of the members of the other team dead, and vice-versa.

1-2 sub-roles per main role are planned to get added later on.

----- SOUND SYSTEM ----

Loud sounds generate directional sound alerts, depending on the player's location.
These can be:
  
  - Gunshots
  - Screams
  - The train's horn
  - Explosions
  etc.

When any of these happen, the player gets a vague, directional notifications. Such as;

  "You hear a gunshot coming from the front of the train"

----- EVIDENCE SYSTEM -----
    
All sorts of crimes leave evidence behind.
Examples:

  - Blood
  - Poison stains / bottles
  - Shell casisngs
  - Fingerprints
    etc.

That tie back to who did the crime, evidence is intentionally imperfect.

----- CORPSES ----

When someone dies, they leave behind a corpse, they do not serve as evidence, but show who died and how.
After roughly a day, these bodies start to decompose, and finish decomposing after 12 hours, leaving behind a unrecognizable skeleton.
The death system works as of the latest update of this documentation, also the corpse part isn't fully refined.

----- ITEM SYSTEM ----

Players each have their own inventory. Items can be found throughout the train, picked up with /take, given to other players with /give, inspected with /inspect, and used through /use. Some items require a target, while others simply display information or perform an action.
Items contain descriptions, lore, or readable text that players may access with /inspect.
Items may be secretly handed to other players, allowing for deception, cooperation, or attempts to frame others.

----- MATCH LENGTH -----

A typical season of TLVOTHE is designed to last
between one and two months depending on player
activity.

Because of this, information, alliances, murders,
and evidence accumulate over long periods of time,
creating an evolving story rather than a short
party game.

A standard season is designed around
24 players.

----- COMMANDS -----

Implemented:

/look
Move between rooms and observe your surroundings.

/move
Travel between connected train cars.

/inventory
View the items you're carrying.

/inspect
Read the description of your item.

/use
Use an item from your inventory.

/give
Give another player an item from your inventory.

/take
Take an item from the room.

/add
(Admin) Add a player to the game.

/remove
(Admin) Remove a player from the game.

/itemgive
(Admin) Give an item to a player.

/itemspawn
(Admin) Spawn an item in a room.

/kill
(Admin) Instantly kill a player.
